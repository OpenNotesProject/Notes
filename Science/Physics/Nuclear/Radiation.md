# Activity
Radioactive activity is defined as the **number of nuclei** decaying **per second**. One **Becquerel is equal to one decay per second**
The Equation for activity is:
$$A=\frac{N}{t}$$
$$\text{Activity (in Bq)}=\frac{\text{No.~of~decays}}{\text{Time~in~s}}$$
###### Example:
Calculate the activity of a source that undergoes 680000 decays in 3 mins
$$A=\frac{N}{t}$$
$$A=\frac{680000}{180}$$
$$A=3778Bq$$
---

# Absorbed Dose
Absorbed dose is the **energy absorbed** per **unit mass** of and absorbing material
$$D=\frac{E}{m}$$
$$\text{Absorbed Dose in Grays (Gy)}=\frac{\text{Energy in Joules(J)}}{\text{Mass in Kg}}$$
###### Example:
A person with a mass of 80 kg is exposed to 500 J of energy from a radioactive source. Calculate the absorbed dose.
$$D=\frac{E}{m}$$
$$D=\frac{500}{80}$$
$$D=6.25Gy$$---
# Equivalent Dose
Equivalent dose combines the absorbed dose with a weighting factor corresponding to the strength of the type of radiation.
$$H=Dw^{}_R$$
$$\text{Equivalent dose rate in Sieverts (Sv)=Absorbed dose (Gy)}*\text{Radiation weighting factor(no~ units)}$$

##### Example:
A body receives 40 mGy of alpha radiation. Calculate the equivalent dose received.
$$H=Dw^{}_R$$$$H=40X10^{-3}X20$$
$$H=0.8Sv$$
---

# Equivalent Dose Rate
Equivalent dose rate takes into account the time exposed to the radiation.
$$\dot{H}=\frac{H}{t}$$
$$\text{Equivalent Dose rate per second,measured in Svs}^{-1}=\frac{\text{Equivilent Dose in Sv}}{\text{Time in s}}$$

##### Example:
A research worker receives 500$\mu Sv$ of radiation over 2 hours.
Calculate the equivalent dose rate for their experiment.
$$\dot{H}=\frac{H}{t}$$
$$\dot{H}=\frac{600X10^{-6}}{2}$$
$$\dot{H}=250X10^{-6}Svh^{-1}$$

A research worker receives 20$\mu Sv$ of radiation over 4 hours.
Calculate the equivalent dose rate for their experiment.
$$\dot{H}=\frac{H}{t}$$
$$\dot{H}=\frac{20X10^{-6}}{4}$$
$$\dot{H}=5X10^{-6}Svh^{-1}$$
