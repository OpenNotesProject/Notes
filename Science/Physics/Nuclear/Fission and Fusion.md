Fission is the splitting of a large nuclei into two small nuclei and energy. Fission is what is used in Nuclear reactors.
Fusion is the joining of two small nuclei to one large nuclei and energy. Fusion is what was used on Hiroshima.

## Nuclear Fission reactors
Energy is released from nuclear fuel in the reactor by a process know as a **chain reaction**, since each fission reaction produces 2 neutrons, you could have an exponential number of neutrons released as each neutron releases 2 neutons. In order to protect the reactor from a meltdown, **control rods** are used to control the reaction by absorbing the loose neutrons that are released.

### Advantages of nuclear reactors 
- Negligible amounts of $\ce{CO2}$ is released
- 1 kg of of Uranium releases 5TJ of energy (1kg of coal releases 28MJ)
- Uranium is hard to get

### Disadvantages of nuclear reactors
- Non-Renewable
- Radioactive waste needs to be stored for thousands of years before it is safe (very expensive)
