# Simplifying
- Identify and **common factors of the numerator or denominator**.
- Factorise the numerator and denominator
- "Cancel" the common factors

## Examples:
1. 
$$\frac{9a}{3}$$
$$=\frac{3*3*a}{3*1}$$
$$=\frac{\cancel3*3*a}{\cancel3*1}$$
$$=\frac{3a}{1}$$

$$=3a$$
2. 
$$\frac{9a}{3a}$$
$$=\frac{3*3*a}{3*a}$$
$$=\frac{\cancel3*3*\cancel{a}}{\cancel3*\cancel{a}}$$
$$=\frac{3}{1}$$
$$=3$$
---
# Simplifying algebraic fractions with brackets
When you simplify and algebraic fraction with brackets, an entire bracket set can be a factor

## Examples:
1. 
$$\frac{x(x+2)}{3(x+2)}$$
$$=\frac{x*(x+2)}{3*(x+2)}$$
$$=\frac{x*\cancel{(x+2)}}{3*\cancel{(x+2)}}$$
$$=\frac{x}{3}$$
2. 
$$\frac{2(a-1)^3}{(a-1)}$$

$$=\frac{2*(a-1)*(a-1)*(a-1)}{(a-1)}$$
$$=\frac{2*\cancel{(a-1)}*(a-1)*(a-1)}{\cancel{(a-1)}}$$
$$=\frac{2*(a-1)^2}{1}$$
$$=2*(a-1)^2$$
3. 
$$\frac{(x-1)(x+1)}{(x-1)^2}$$
$$=\frac{(x-1)(x+1)}{(x-1)(x-1)}$$
$$=\frac{\cancel{(x-1)}(x+1)}{\cancel{(x-1)}(x-1)}$$
$$=\frac{x+1}{x-1}$$
---

# Simplifying algebraic fractions by factorising first
To simplify and algebraic fraction:
- **First fully factorise** the numerator and denominator
- **Then cancel any common factors** of the numerator and denominator

## Examples:
1. 
$$\frac{6x-9}{3}$$
$$=\frac{3(2x-3)}{3}$$
$$=\frac{\cancel3(2x-3)}{\cancel3}$$$$=\frac{2x-3}{1}$$$$=2x-3$$
2.  
$$\frac{5}{15x+10}$$
$$=\frac{5}{5(3x+2)}$$
$$=\frac{\cancel5}{\cancel5(3x+2)}$$
$$=\frac{1}{(3x+2)}$$
3. $$\frac{2x+6}{2x-8}$$
$$=\frac{2(x+3)}{2(x-4)}$$
$$=\frac{\cancel2(x+3)}{\cancel2(x-4)}$$
$$=\frac{(x+3)}{(x-4)}$$
$$\text{Cannot be factorised further}$$
4. $$\frac{3x-6}{(x-2)(x+2)}$$
$$=\frac{3(x-2)}{(x-2)(x+2)}$$
$$=\frac{3\cancel{(x-2)}}{\cancel{(x-2)}(x+2)}$$
$$=\frac{3}{(x+2)}$$
5. $$\frac{4x^2-8x}{x^2-x-2}$$
$$=\frac{4x(x-2)}{(x+1)(x-2)}$$
$$=\frac{4x\cancel{(x-2)}}{(x+1)\cancel{(x-2)}}$$
$$=\frac{4x}{(x+1)}$$
---
# Multiplying algebraic fractions
To multiply an algebraic fraction:
- Use the same method as for numerical fractions
- Make sure your final answer is in **its simplest form** 

## Examples:
1. $$\frac{x}{2}\times\frac{x}{5}$$
$$=\frac{x^2}{10}$$
2. $$\frac{5a}{4}\times\frac{6}{a^2}$$
$$=\frac{30a}{4a^2}$$
$$=\frac{\cancel{30}15a}{\cancel42a^2}$$
$$=\frac{15\cancel{a}\times1}{2\cancel{a^2}a}$$
$$=\frac{15}{2a}$$
3. $$\frac{8y^3}{3x}\times\frac{9x^2}{4y}$$
$$=\frac{8y^3\times9x^2}{3x\times4y}$$
$$=\frac{\cancel82y^3\times9x^2}{3x\times\cancel41y}$$
$$=\frac{2y^3\times\cancel93x^2}{\cancel3x\times1y}$$
$$=\frac{6y^3x^2}{xy}$$
$$=\frac{6\cancel{y^3}y^2x^2}{x\cancel{y}}$$
$$=\frac{6y^2\cancel{x^2}x}{\cancel{x}}$$
$$=\frac{6y^2x}{1\times1}$$
$$=6y^2x$$
---
# Dividing Algebraic fractions
To divide algebraic fractions, use the same method as numerical fractions: Flip the fraction then multiply

## Examples:
1. $$\frac{x}{2}\div\frac{x^2}{5}$$
$$\frac{x}{2}\times\frac{5}{x^2}$$
$$=\frac{5x}{2x^2}$$
$$=\frac{5\cancel{x}1}{2\cancel{x^2}x}$$
$$=\frac{5}{2x}$$
2. $$\frac{5t^2}{4b}\div\frac{3t}{b^3}$$
$$=\frac{5t^2}{4b}\times\frac{b^3}{3t}$$
$$=\frac{5t^2b^3}{12bt}$$
$$=\frac{5\cancel{t^2}b^3}{12b\cancel{t}}$$
$$=\frac{5t\cancel{b^3}}{12\cancel{b}}$$
---
# Adding and subtracting algebraic fractions
To add and subtract algebraic fractions, use the same method as with numerical fractions, make the denominators the same

## Examples:
1. $$\frac{a}{2}-\frac{a}{3}$$
$$\text{LCM=6}$$
$$\text{Left Side}\times3~~~\text{Right side}\times2$$
$$=\frac{3a}{6}-\frac{2a}{6}$$
$$\frac{a}{6}$$
2. $$\frac{3}{x}+\frac{5}{y}$$
$$\text{LCM=}xy$$
$$\text{Left Side}\times{y}~~~\text{Right Side}\times{x}$$
$$=\frac{3y}{xy}+\frac{5x}{xy}$$
$$=\frac{3y+5x}{xy}$$
3. $$\frac{2}{3m}-\frac{1}{6m}$$
$$\text{LCM=}6mn$$
$$\text{Left side}\times2n~~~\text{Left Side}\times{m}$$
$$=\frac{4n}{6mn}-\frac{m}{6mn}$$
$$\frac{4n-m}{6mn}$$
### More Complex examples
1. $$\frac{x+1}{2}+\frac{x-3}{4}$$
$$=\frac{(x+1)}{2}+\frac{(x-3)}{4}$$
$$\text{LCM}=4$$
$$=\frac{2(x+1)}{4}+\frac{(x-3)}{4}$$
$$=\frac{2(x+1)+(x-3)}{4}$$
$$=\frac{2x+2+x-3}{4}$$
$$=\frac{3x-1}{4}$$
2. $$\frac{2}{p+1}+\frac{3}{p-2}$$
$$=\frac{2}{(p+1)}+\frac{3}{(p-2)}$$
$$\text{LCM=}(p+1)\times(p-2)$$
$$=\frac{2(p-2)}{(p+1)(p-2)}+\frac{3(p+1)}{(p-2)(p+1)}$$
$$=\frac{2(p-2)+3(p+1)}{(p+1)\times(p-2)}$$
$$=\frac{2p-4+3p+3}{(p+1)(p-2)}$$
$$=\frac{5p-1}{(p+1)(p-2)}$$
