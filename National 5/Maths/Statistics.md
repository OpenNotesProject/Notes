# Averages
There are three types of average: Mean, Median and Mode
- Mean
	- $\frac{Sum~of~all~values}{No~of~values}$ 
- Median
	- Middle Value after putting all the values in order
- Mode 
	- The number that appears most often

# The range of a set of data 
The range of a piece of data is the highest point - the lowest point of data.
The range of a set of data tells us how spread out the data is.

# Quartiles
The **Median** divides a set of data into **two parts**:
The **lower half** and the **upper half**. 

The **lower quartile (Q1)** is the median of the lower half.
The **upper quartile (Q3)** is the median of the upper half.
**Q2** is the median of the whole data set


> [!NOTE] 
> The **Quartiles** divide a dataset into quarters - hence the word **Quart**ile

### Examples:
1  2  3 **|** 4  5  5  **6**  7  7  8 **|** 8  9  10
     **Q1**          **Q2**          **Q3**
 Q2 = Median = 7th value 
 Q2 = 6
 Q1 = 3.5
 Q3 = 8

---
8  9  10 **|** 10  11  12 **|** 13  13  14 **|** 16 16 18
Q2 = Median = Between 6th and 7th value
Q2 = 12.5
Q1 = 10
Q3 = 15

## Interquartile range 
Interquartile range = Q3 - Q1
The IQR describes the range/how spread out the **middle 50% of the data set** is.
The **smaller the IQR** the **more similar or consistent** the middle 50% of the data set is.
### Example:
The weights of a group of 15-year-old boys was recorded in kg:
60  60  62 **|** 64  65  65 **|** 66  69  70 **|** 71  72  83

$Q1=63$
$Q2 = 65.5$
$Q3 = 70.5$ 
$$IQR=70.5-63$$
$$IQR=7.5$$
---

# Standard deviation
The standard deviation of a data set is another way to **measure the spread of a data set.**
It calculates **how much, on average**, the data values **deviate from the mean**.
Standard deviation can be calculated with this formula.
$$S=\sqrt{\frac{\Sigma(x-\overline{x})^2}{n-1}}$$
$$\Sigma=\text{Stands for sum of}$$
$$S=\sqrt{\frac{\text{Sum of }(x-\overline{x}^2)}{\text{Number of values}-1}}$$
### Examples:
#### Without the Formula
*Calculate the standard deviation of this data set* 
4  9  10  12  15
1. Find the mean/$\overline{x}$ of the values
$$\overline{x}=\frac{\text{sum}}{5}$$
$$\overline{x}= \frac{50}{5}$$
$$\overline{x}=10$$
2. Set up a table

| $x$ | $x-\overline{x}$ | $(x-\overline{x})^2$ |
| --- | ---------------- | -------------------- |
| 4   | -6               | 36                   |
| 9   | -1               | 1                    |
| 10  | 0                | 0                    |
| 12  | 2                | 4                    |
| 15  | 5                | 25                   |
|     | Sum =            | 66                   |
3. **Divide** the Sum by **one less than** the number of values and take the square root
no of values = 5
$$\text{s.d.}=\sqrt{\frac{66}{4}}$$
$$\text{s.d.}=\sqrt{16.5}$$
$$\text{s.d.}=4.062(\text{3dp})$$
#### With the formula:
*A runner has recorded her time in seconds for 6 different laps of a running track*
55  57  58  60  55  56
1. Calculate the mean of the track times
$$\overline{x}=\frac{341}{6}$$
$$\overline{x}=56.83\text{s}$$
2. Make the table

| $x$ | $x-\overline{x}$ | $(x-\overline{x})^2$ |
| --- | ---------------- | -------------------- |
| 55  | -1.8             | 3.24                 |
| 57  | 0.2              | 0.04                 |
| 58  | 1.2              | 1.44                 |
| 60  | 3.2              | 10.24                |
| 55  | -1.8             | 3.24                 |
| 56  | -0.8             | 0.46                 |
|     | Sum/$\Sigma$ =   | 18.84                |
$$S=\sqrt{\frac{\Sigma(x-\overline{x})^2}{n-1}}$$
$$\Sigma(x-\overline{x})^2=18.84$$
$$\text{S}=\sqrt{\frac{18.84}{6-1}}$$
$$\text{S}=\sqrt{\frac{18.84}{5}}$$
$$\text{S}=\sqrt{3.768}$$
$$\text{S}=1.9411$$
$$\text{S}=1.94\text{s}$$
