# How can people be involved in the Democratic System?

## One way that you can participate in politics is:

### Joining a Political party
Joining a political party is a easy way to join a group of people who share your beliefs and ideologies about how the country should be run.
It allows you to attend local meetings and the annual party conference.

By joining a political party you can:
- Take part in selecting party policies, candidates and the party leader.
- Labour Party members were able to vote to elect Keir Starmer in 2020 to replace Jeremy Corbyn as leader.

---

### Standing as a Candidate
**People can stand as a candidate for a party with the hopes of becoming a representative.**
Usually, people stand for a political party, e.g. SNP or Labour, but they can also run **independently**

> [!Explain question example:]
> For example, if you are over 18 and a British citizen without a criminal record, you can stand for election as an MP, MSP or councillor.


> [!NOTE] Example of Participation
>  Labour's Sam Carling 22-year-old was elected as an MP with a razor-thin majority (39 votes) taking a Long-standing Labour seat, held by MP Shailesh Vara, in the 2024 election.

##### Why do people not vote? 

###### Reason 1: Voting is not *compulsory* in the UK
- In several countries, voting is compulsory, e.g. in South Australia you will be fined for not voting
- In the UK it is seen as a clear right to decide not to vote; it's up to the parties, to persuade you, and to generate interest in politics and to get people to go and vote

###### Reason 2: "Voter Apathy"
- Some people have **lost interest** in politics, This often leads to them not voting/registering to vote. 
- However, the belief that voting **does not make any real difference to the problems people are facing** have been identified as reasons for low levels or participation in elections.

###### Reason 3: Dissatisfaction and Lack of trust
- Over the past two decades it has become clear that many people have lost trust and respect of the "political elite"/"Ruling Class" who, it is argued, to have done a terrible job of running the country.
- The MP's Expenses Scandal
	- FOIA was being passed and the MP's wanted to stop it form applying to MP's. This caused people to want to look into them.

###### Reason 4: "Safe Seats"
- If you are a voter in a "Safe Seat" constituency you are unlikely to vote for another party, because you feel that they cannot win.
