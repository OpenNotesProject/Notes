nileCampaigning is the process of convincing people to vote for you, rather than the other candidates. Campaigning enables parties to get their message across, **target voters**, **raise** **issues**, attack and outsmart opponents all with the hope of retaining support and attracting new supporters.
Their are **various types of campaign methods** that candidates and parties will use.
## Campaign methods

###  Traditional Strategies
#### Canvasing (People coming to your door)
- Party members will go around door to door and engage in conversation
- Allows Parties to have a direct connection with voters
#### Posters
- Memorable Slogans
	- Example - Labour 2019:
	  "For the many
	    Not the few"
- Parties usually hire Advertising Agencies for professional advice on how to get their posters 'noticed' or to ensure the posters have the biggest or desired impact.
#### Party Political Broadcasts
- If you get more than 5% of the vote in the previous election then the government will fund a PPB
- Allows the Party to easily send a direct message to a large number of people in the country(everyone watching terrestrial TV)
	- Example - Labour PPB May 2023:
		- Blaming the conservatives for the cost of living crisis, say Labour will fix it.
		- Said they would Double the number of land-based wind turbines
	- Example - Tory PPB 2024:
		- They are Deporting a bunch of immigrants???
		- Russia is bad
		- Only the Torys will be the ones that will make a secure future for Britain
		- They did the Rwanda scheme (because that was so good)
		- Delayed HS2???
		- Made 18-year old's do community service or be in the army????
		- Labour don't want to increase Defence spending 
		- Labour want good workers rights ???
	- Example - Green Party PPB 2024
		- Would invest the most money in the NHS

### Contemporary Strategies
#### Use of Social Media
- Social media allows political parties to directly communicate with large numbers of voters and tell them about their policies.
- Enables targeting of specific audiences
- According to YouGov, 1 in 3 voters (32%) follow a political party on social media.
##### For example:
In the 2019 General Election, the first election campaign videos by Labour and the Conservatives on Twitter/X received over 4 million views, as detailed by Cardiff University.
