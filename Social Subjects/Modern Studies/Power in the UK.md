Power is separated in UK politics to make sure that no one element of the government gets to powerful.

**Executive** - The Government, e.g. PM, the PM's Cabinet and the Civil Service
**Legislative** - know as Parliament, these are the law makers e.g. House of Commons and House of Lords
**Judicial** - the Courts, they decide the laws and who has broken them.

## Powers of the PM
- They are the main government spokesperson
	- Makes all the big announcements on be half of the government
	- He is often questioned on TV and debate shows
	- They are responsible for all decisions that the government makes, they might not make them all but they are to blame!
- They represent the UK abroad
	- Have to go and visit other countries diplomatically
		- e.g. went to china
		- went to Ukraine 
	- Go to Negotiate with other countries
		- Go to G8 
		- Go to China, Get visa deal
	- Example:
		- The UK left the **EU** on the 31st of January 2020, and the year-long **transition period** ended on the last day of December 2020. ==FINISH THIS, ITS IN THE SLIDES==
- They have the power of "patronage"
	- The PM has the power to Appoint(hire), dismiss (fire) or reshuffle(move) their cabinet ministers
	- Examples who have been sacked:
		- Peter Mandelson
- They control **Government Business**
	- They decide what is discussed in parliament and what the government is working on
	- They decide what the Cabinet discuss
	- e.g.
		- Cost of living
		- Gaza and Ukraine
		- "The Boats"
		- China as a threat to the UK
		- War in the middle east
